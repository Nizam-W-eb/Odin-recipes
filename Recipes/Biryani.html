Biryani

Biryani is a popular dish in India it's very delicious item and every person who comes to India should try it.

there are various types in them like Chicken Biryani , Mutton Birayani etc. Birayani is made of meat, vegitables and fluffy basmati rice and today you are going to know about chicken birayani.

Ingridients Need To Make Biryani

  • 4 cups basmati rice
  • 3 tablespoons Ginger & garlic paste
  • 5 green chilies (or less, depending on taste)
  • 1 onion finely chopped
  • 2 teaspoons ea. cinnamon, cloves, cardamom
  • Cashew nuts
  • 4 tablespoons oil or ghee
  • 2 cups carrot, peas, & finely cut beans
  • 2 teaspoons Garam Masala powder
  • 3 teaspoons chili powder (or less, depending on taste)
  • Mint leaves & coriander leaves (handful)
  • Juice of ½ lemon

Making the Biryani

  1. Wash the basmati rice
  2. Soak the rice.
  3. Chop the vegetables.
  4. Heat oil in a pan over medium heat. Add the cloves, cardamom, and cinnamon to the pan. Sauté for a few seconds, then add onions. Sauté until the onions turn transparent.
  5. Add mint leaves, coriander leaves, and green chilies to the pan. Stir and Sauté for a minute or so. Then add the ginger and garlic paste. Stir into the mixture in the pan, then Sauté for a couple of minutes.
  6. Add garam masala, chili powder, carrots, peas and beans. Sauté for a few more minutes, stirring regularly.
  7. Pour in 8 cups of water. After adding the water, add salt to taste. Stir everything together, then bring the mixture to a boil.
  8. Add the rice. Pour the rice into the boiling water. Add the lemon juice. Cover the pot with a tight lid. Let the rice cook until done. The rice is done when it is al dente, not mushy. As you check the rice, refrain from stirring. This will break the rice grains. Add more water to the pot if you believe the water level is getting too low. Recover the rice and continue letting it cook.
  9. Serve. Once the rice has cooked, serve the biryani hot. Try serving the biryani rice with rich curries or other delectable Indian main courses.
